01 About

As AI systems evolve toward human-like intelligence, the ability to reason across multiple modalities‚Äîvision, language, and beyond‚Äîhas become a central challenge. This workshop brings together researchers exploring the frontiers of multimodal reasoning, from algorithmic and mathematical problem-solving to physical understanding and agentic behavior.

Multimodal Reasoning

Advancing algorithmic, mathematical, and physical reasoning capabilities across vision, language, and other modalities in foundation models.

AI vs. Human Cognition

Comparative studies between AI and human problem-solving, drawing insights from psychology, neuroscience, and cognitive development.

Agentic AI Systems

Multi-agent collaboration, distributed problem solving, and autonomous decision-making in complex environments.

Extreme Generalization

Few-shot concept induction, transfer to novel tasks, and understanding the limits of reasoning in large-scale models.

Scientific Applications

Multimodal reasoning for scientific discovery, hypothesis generation, and complex problem-solving in research domains.

Scaling & Efficiency

Scaling laws for reasoning, efficient test-time computation, and algorithmic improvements for enhanced inference.

This workshop aims to bridge the gap between current AI capabilities and human-like reasoning by fostering interdisciplinary dialogue on multimodal intelligence. We seek to understand how models represent and process algorithms, why they fail, and how they can be improved. By bringing together perspectives from machine learning, cognitive science, neuroscience, and domain sciences, we aim to chart a path toward AI systems capable of robust, generalizable reasoning across diverse tasks and modalities.

02 Call for Papers

We invite submissions exploring all aspects of multimodal reasoning in AI systems. We welcome novel algorithms, empirical studies, theoretical analyses, position papers, and work-in-progress research that advances our understanding of how AI systems reason across modalities.

Submission Guidelines

  • Format: All submissions must be in PDF format using the ICML 2026 LaTeX style file.
  • Double-Blind: The reviewing process will be double blind. Anonymize your submission.
  • Dual-Submission: We welcome ongoing and unpublished works. Papers under review elsewhere are allowed.
  • Non-Archival: The workshop is non-archival. Submissions can be submitted to other venues.
